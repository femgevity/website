The Care Gap

Women's midlife health is the largest underserved category in medicine.

Hormonal change, metabolic shift, and longevity-relevant biomarkers move quietly through a woman's 30s, 40s, and 50s — often years before symptoms drive a clinical visit. The current model catches these changes late, treats them in silos, and leaves significant clinical and economic value on the table.

i.

Fragmented Specialty Care

Hormones, metabolic health, and longevity are managed across disconnected specialties. Patients navigate gaps. Providers lose visibility into the full picture.

ii.

Reactive vs. Longitudinal Care

Episodic visits miss the early-intervention window when biomarker shifts are most actionable. Longitudinal care delivers better outcomes at lower long-term cost.

iii.

Unrealized Reimbursement & Risk

Reimbursable diagnostic and management services go unbilled. Preventable escalations drive avoidable downstream cost across both provider P&Ls and payer risk pools.

70 million+ women in the U.S. are entering or in menopause. Lost workdays and unmanaged menopause-related health needs cost the U.S. an estimated $24.8 billion annually. Closing the women's midlife care gap could add over $1 trillion to global GDP by 2040.

Sources: U.S. Census Bureau; Mayo Clinic Proceedings; McKinsey Health Institute, 2024
